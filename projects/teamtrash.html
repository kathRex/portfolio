// Entertainment Computing

Team Trash

Game / 2D / Platformer / Interaction Design / Pixel Art · 2025

↓ Download Game

Team Trash is a 2D platformer set in a neon-drenched city and its shadowy sewer system, where players freely switch between a witty raccoon and a semi-aggressive possum on a chaotic trash-scavenging adventure.
The game revolves around a simple dilemma: collect trash to improve your chances of survival, or spend it as ammunition against the dangers lurking throughout the city. As players explore, fight enemies and gather resources, their decisions shape a dynamic ending that reflects both their survival and the choices they made along the way.

// Core Systems

🦝 Character Switching

  • Instant switching between raccoon and possum
  • Shared world state with individual health values
  • Two-character survival setup built around teamwork

🍕 Resource System: Trash = Ammo = Score

  • Collected trash increases the final score
  • Trash can be thrown as projectiles but reduces the score
  • Health is displayed as a pizza wheel with four slices per character

This creates constant tension between greed and safety.


🌆 Dynamic Threat Zones

  • Sewer - toxic rats, polluted water, moving platforms and spike traps
  • City - robo-cats with chase behaviour, laser attacks and environmental hazards
  • Boss Encounter - giant robo-cat as the final challenge

🎭 Special Abilities

  • Possum - can enter a “Possum Motel”, returns with possum babies and can use them as distraction grenades with narrative consequences if abandoned
  • Raccoon - can collect a strawberry power-up, visually gains a strawberry hat and temporarily shoots unlimited trash without reducing the score

✨ Dynamic Outcome System

The ending screen dynamically changes based on total trash collected, survival of characters, whether possum abandoned its babies and the player’s greed-vs-survival balance.

// Technical Implementation

Systems, AI & Visual Identity

The project was developed in Unity using a custom gameplay framework built around character switching, resource tracking and enemy behaviour. Enemy encounters rely on detection systems, pursuit logic, melee and ranged attacks that react dynamically to player actions.
Alongside programming the game systems, I created the complete visual presentation myself, including character sprites, animations, environment assets, UI elements and level artwork.


Technical Highlights

  • Custom character-switching and health management system
  • Resource tracking tied directly to combat mechanics
  • Enemy AI with raycast-based player detection and pursuit behaviour
  • Ranged enemy attacks using line-rendered laser systems and combat state logic
  • Event-driven gameplay and environmental hazards
  • Complete pixel art production, including characters, animations, tilesets and UI
